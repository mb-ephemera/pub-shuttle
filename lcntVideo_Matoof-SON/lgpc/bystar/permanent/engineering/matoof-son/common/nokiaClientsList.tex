\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{T-Mobile USA}
  \item \textbf{Telstra} (Australia)
  \item \textbf{Orange} (Global/Europe/MEA)
  \item \textbf{Vodafone Idea} (India)
  \item \textbf{Telecom Argentina}
  \item \textbf{BT (EE)} (UK)
  \item \textbf{NTT DOCOMO} (Japan)
\end{itemize}

And probably many other telecom companies that have not been publicly disclosed.

\pnote{
  Nokia EdenNet SON is not a niche product. It is deployed at scale across the globe.

  Based on public announcements and reports, the known customer list includes
  T-Mobile in the United States, Telstra in Australia, Orange across Europe and
  the Middle East, Vodafone Idea in India, Telecom Argentina, BT and EE in the
  United Kingdom, and NTT DOCOMO in Japan.

  These are among the largest telecom operators in the world. And there are almost
  certainly many others that have not been publicly disclosed.

  Every one of these operators has SON modules. Every one of them runs those modules
  through a single player --- the Nokia Web UI. And every one of them would benefit
  from soncli.mcs.

  The opportunity here is not small.

  \pnote{
  Who is this presentation for?

  The table on this slide maps four types of audience against four areas of relevance.

  The most directly relevant audience is Nokia SON Customers --- operators like
  T-Mobile and others on the previous slide. If you develop SON modules, if you run
  them, or if you are responsible for RAN automation, soncli.mcs is directly
  applicable to your work.

  Nokia itself is also an audience --- because soncli.mcs changes the competitive
  dynamics around their platform.

  Telecom operators who do not currently use Nokia SON --- for example those using
  AT&T's own RAN stack or other vendors --- are a secondary audience. The MaTOOF
  concepts that underlie soncli.mcs apply broadly to systems management.

  Finally, internet providers and cloud operators are an audience for the broader
  BISOS-MaTOOF framework, even if Nokia SON is not directly relevant to them.

  If you are watching this, you are likely in one of these groups.
